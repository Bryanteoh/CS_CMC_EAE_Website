About Me

认识我 · Get to know me

个人简介 Chinese language · culture · storytelling

I am a reflective and responsible student who enjoys using Chinese to observe everyday life, express emotions and share stories that help people understand one another.

Publishing Chinese articles showed me that language is more than a school subject. It can record memories, explore social experiences and help readers feel understood. Cultural activities also taught me to look beyond surface traditions and consider how culture continues to change in Singapore. I hope to strengthen my cultural knowledge while becoming a clearer writer, speaker and bilingual storyteller.

肆 · 04

My Growth Journey

从兴趣到方向 · How my interest became a clear direction

Step 1
Discovering My Interest

Seeing Chinese Beyond the Classroom

I began to see Chinese not only as a subject, but as a way to record experiences, express emotions and understand the people and culture around me. This gave me the confidence to write with greater purpose.

Growth: curiosity, reflection, self-expression
Step 2
Published Writing

Learning to Write for Real Readers

Through six published Chinese pieces, I learnt to identify a meaningful angle, organise ideas clearly and revise my language carefully. Publishing also taught me to consider how different readers may understand and respond to a story.

Growth: Chinese writing, editing, audience awareness
Step 3
Cultural Participation

Learning Beyond the Page

Activities such as the ZB Schools exhibition, NTU cultural camp and annual correspondent orientation allowed me to learn through participation. They encouraged me to observe more carefully, communicate with others and reflect on how culture is shared in present-day Singapore.

Growth: cultural awareness, observation, communication
Step 4
Moving Forward

Turning Experience into Direction

My CCA records show steady growth across leadership, achievement, participation and service. Roles such as Environment Advocate, CCA Executive Committee member and Section/Group Leader strengthened my responsibility, while choir, competitions and VIA projects helped me build teamwork, confidence and commitment.

Growth: leadership, service, teamwork, responsibility

This journey shows how an interest in Chinese language gradually became a clear goal: to understand culture more deeply and communicate meaningful stories with accuracy, empathy and creativity.
